    Time of Event

        • Definition
        • Context
        • Examples
        • Related Terms

    Definition #

    Time of event refers to the specific point in time at which a defined event occurs in a clinical, observational, or healthcare setting. This could relate to symptom onset, treatment administration, exposure, or diagnosis.

    Context #

    Accurate recording of time-of-event is essential for temporal reasoning, causal modeling, and clinical trial protocol adherence. It enables analysis of time-dependent variables and outcomes (e.g., survival analysis, time-to-event modeling).

    In observational studies, knowing the exact time of exposure or outcome event supports time-alignment and confounding control.

    Examples #

    • Time a drug was administered to a patient
    • Timestamp of symptom onset in an EHR
    • Exact time of adverse event reporting in a clinical trial
